About Me

My name is Mostafa H. Deldoost. I am an interdisciplinary researcher in neuroeconomics(combining neuroscience, psychology, and economics), and behavioral economics. I am passionate about understanding what happens in the brain when we make decisions. I use EEG and eye-tracking techniques to connect neural activity with economic behavior, exploring how humans think and choose.

News & Updates

  • Mar 2025 Paper Submitted: "Beyond Aggregation: How Microeconomic Heterogeneity Shapes Macroeconomic Emergence" to the Journal of Interdisciplinary Economics.

    This work challenges traditional 'representative agent' models by applying complexity theory. Using the Ising model from statistical mechanics, we demonstrate how micro-heterogeneity, social interactions, and external factors lead to unexpected, emergent behaviors in collective social decision-making.
